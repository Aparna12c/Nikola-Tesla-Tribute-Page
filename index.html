Timeline 1856 - 1882

~ 1856 ~

Born in Smiljan, Croatia (Austrian Empire)

~ 1861 ~

Tesla started primary school, where he studied German, Arithmetic and Religion.

~ 1862 ~

The Tesla family moved to Gospić, Lika. Here Nikola completed Primary School, followed by Middle School.

~ 1870 ~

Nikola moved to Karlovac, to attend High School at the Higher Real Gymnasium, which was a school within the Austro-Hungarian Military Frontier.

~ 1873 ~

Tesla graduated from the Gymnasium, finishing 4 years worth of study in 3 years.

After graduation he returned to Smiljan where soon he became sick with Cholera, many times near death, Tesla's father promised him if he recovered, he would send Nikola to the best engineering school

~ 1874 ~

Tesla evaded conscription into the Astro-Hungarian Army by moving to Tomingaj, near Gračac.

~ 1875 ~

Tesla enrolled at Austrian Polytechnic in Graz, Austria in a Military Frontier Scholarship. In his first year, Tesla earned the highest grades possible, never missing a lecture.

During his second year, Nikola lost his scholarship and became addicted to gambling. During his third year he continued gambling, only later he gambled back his losses and returned the balance to his family.

Wehn examination time came, Nikola was unprepared and asked for an extension to study, he was denied.

~ 1878 ~

Nikola stopped all communications with his family and moved to Maribor to hide the fact that he dropped out of school.

In Maribor, he worked as a draftsman.

~ 1879 ~

In March, Tesla's father wen't to Maribor begging his son to come home. Nikola refused, and was also suffering a nervous breakdown.

In April, Milutin Tesla (Nikola's Father) passed away.

~ 1880 ~

Two of Nikola's uncles put together enough money to help him leave Gospić, to Prague where he was to study at the Charles-Ferdinand University.

Unfortunately his enrolment was not successful, however he still attended many lectures of Philosophy as an auditor, therefore he did not receive any grades for these.

~ 1881 ~

Tesla moved to Budapest, Hungary to work at the Budapest Telephone Exchange. .
